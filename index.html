First Aid
✅What TO DO During Active Seizure▶
🟢
Protect from injury — clear hard/sharp objects, cushion the head
🟢
Time the seizure — >5 min = status epilepticus threshold → call EMS
🟢
Recovery position (lateral decubitus) — after convulsive phase ends, or immediately if on their side already
🟢
Loosen tight clothing around neck and chest
🟢
Stay with the patient until fully recovered
⚡
Moving the patient: Only if in immediate danger (road, water, stairs). Drag by armpits as a unit — minimal handling — then resume positioning.
🚫What NOT TO DO▶
🔴
Do NOT restrain movements — forced restraint causes injuries
🔴
Nothing in the mouth — tongue swallowing is a myth; object injury is real
🔴
No water or food during or immediately after
🔴
Do NOT aggressively reposition unless danger is immediate
🔴
Do NOT leave alone — monitor until full recovery

💓CPR — Ventilation First Algorithm▶
RESPONSIVE + breathing? → Lateral decubitus (recovery position) → Remove wet clothing, keep warm → Monitor · activate EMS UNRESPONSIVE or abnormal breathing? → 5 RESCUE BREATHS FIRST → Check pulse → No pulse: CPR 30:2 → AED as soon as available
💡
Ventilation is the priority. Drowning = hypoxic arrest. Start with 5 rescue breaths before compressions — unlike standard cardiac arrest.
ER — ABCDE
🔍Primary Survey▶
A
Airway
Patent? Vomit, debris, laryngospasm? GCS ≤8 or unprotected → intubate early. Do not wait for deterioration.
B
Breathing
SpO₂, RR, work of breathing. Auscultate — crackles = pulmonary edema/aspiration. CXR immediately. Target SpO₂ >94%, avoid hyperoxia.
C
Circulation
Hypothermia → bradycardia, arrhythmias, hypotension. 12-lead ECG — Osborn (J) waves? IV ×2, warm fluids if hypothermic.
D
Disability
GCS, pupils, blood glucose.
E
Exposure
Core temperature — rectal or esophageal. Full skin exam for trauma. Remove all wet clothing immediately.
Pulmonary
🫁Pulmonary Management▶
→
ARDS protocol if intubated — 6 ml/kg IBW, PEEP titration
→
CPAP / HFNC bridge for moderate hypoxia before intubation
→
Surfactant washout is the core insult — treat as ARDS
→
Antibiotics NOT routine — only for contaminated water or infection signs
→
Serial CXR at 4–6 hr — pulmonary edema can evolve after normal initial film
⚠️
Secondary drowning is real. Delayed pulmonary edema can be fatal. Never discharge without ≥6–8 hours observation, even Grade 1.

💡Key Clinical Pearls▶
◆
"Not dead until warm and dead" — continue resuscitation after cold-water submersion until core temp ≥32–35°C
◆
Cold water is neuroprotective — good neuro outcomes still possible after extended submersion
◆
C-spine only if indicated — witnessed dive/trauma only. Routine immobilization delays CPR
◆
Salt vs fresh water — minimal acute difference; both cause surfactant washout and ARDS pattern
Swiss Grading
🌡️Hypothermia Grades — Swiss System▶
HT I 32–35°C Mild — Conscious, shivering present
HT II 28–32°C Moderate — Impaired consciousness, shivering stops
HT III 24–28°C Severe — Unconscious, vital signs present
HT IV <24°C Arrest — No vital signs
HT V Irreversible Death from hypothermia confirmed
💡
Key sign: Shivering stops at ~30–32°C. A quiet, non-shivering patient is not reassuring — they may have crossed into moderate territory.
Rewarming
🎯Target Temperature & Rate▶
Target Temp
36–37.5°C
Normothermia — avoid overshooting
Rewarm Rate
~1°C/hr
Moderate–severe hypothermia
⚠️
Too fast → rewarming shock (peripheral vasodilation → distributive shock) and afterdrop. Always warm trunk first, not extremities.
♨️Strategy by Grade▶
HT I · 32–35°C
Passive — blankets, warm environment, remove wet clothing
HT II · 28–32°C
Active external — warming blankets to trunk, warm humidified O₂, warm IV fluids 38–42°C
HT III · 24–28°C
Active internal — warm IV fluids, warm O₂, body cavity lavage (gastric, bladder, pleural, peritoneal)
HT IV · <24°C
ECMO / ECPR — extracorporeal rewarming; most effective in cardiac arrest
Post-Arrest TTM
🧊Targeted Temperature Management▶
Post-ROSC · comatose
32–36°C for 24 hr → controlled rewarming
Active hypothermic arrest
Rewarm to normothermia first → reassess neuro when warm
Avoid post-ROSC
>37.5°C — hyperthermia is clearly harmful
📖
TTM2 Trial (2021): Shifted to 36°C rather than 33°C for most post-arrest patients. Key goal = prevent hyperthermia, not chase a cold target.
Clinical Pitfalls
⚠️Critical Traps▶
Afterdrop
Core temp continues falling after rewarming starts — cold peripheral blood floods core. Don't panic; keep rewarming.
Rewarming arrhythmias
VF risk highest during rewarming in HT III–IV. Gentle handling, minimal stimulation, continuous ECG monitoring.
K⁺ >12 mEq/L
Futility marker — irreversible cell death. Consider stopping CPR/ECMO in hypothermic arrest.
Declaring death too early
"Not dead until warm and dead" — do not declare until core temp ≥32–35°C has been achieved.
Osborn (J) waves
Pathognomonic but not always present. Don't exclude hypothermia by ECG alone.
Rewarming shock
Aggressive peripheral warming → vasodilation → distributive shock. Always warm trunk first.
Impaired drug metabolism
Hypothermia slows clearance — avoid stacking drugs. Many won't work until the patient is rewarmed.
